# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

The Deadbelt Operations Platform (DOP) is committed to fostering an open, welcoming, respectful, and collaborative community.

We believe that great software is built by people with different backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives working together toward a common goal.

As contributors, maintainers, and community members, we pledge to make participation in our project a positive experience for everyone.

---

# Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive community include:

* Being respectful and professional.
* Assuming good intent.
* Providing constructive feedback.
* Being open to different viewpoints.
* Accepting responsibility for mistakes.
* Helping new contributors learn.
* Focusing discussions on improving the project.
* Celebrating the success of others.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment or discrimination of any kind.
* Personal attacks or insults.
* Trolling or deliberately disruptive behavior.
* Publishing another person's private information without permission.
* Intimidation or threats.
* Bad-faith discussions intended to derail productive collaboration.
* Any behavior that would reasonably make another community member feel unwelcome or unsafe.

---

# Project Philosophy

Deadbelt exists because we believe powerful infrastructure software should be open, collaborative, and accessible.

Disagreements are expected.

Disrespect is not.

Engineering decisions should be debated on their technical merits, supported by evidence, testing, and thoughtful discussion.

---

# Community Expectations

We encourage contributors to:

* Ask questions.
* Share ideas.
* Challenge assumptions respectfully.
* Support one another.
* Help improve documentation.
* Mentor new contributors.
* Learn continuously.

Every contributor, regardless of experience level, deserves to be treated with professionalism and respect.

---

# Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

* Clarifying community expectations.
* Addressing unacceptable behavior.
* Acting fairly and consistently.
* Protecting the health of the community.

Maintainers are expected to lead by example.

---

# Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all official Deadbelt project spaces, including but not limited to:

* GitHub repositories
* GitHub Discussions
* Discord server
* Documentation
* Issue discussions
* Pull Requests
* Community events
* Any official communication channels

---

# Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it privately to the project maintainers.

Reports should include:

* A description of the incident.
* Relevant links or screenshots (if available).
* Any additional context that may assist in reviewing the report.

Reports will be handled respectfully and, where practical, confidentially.

---

# Enforcement Guidelines

When responding to unacceptable behavior, maintainers may take actions including:

* Private warning.
* Request to modify behavior.
* Temporary removal from discussions.
* Temporary suspension.
* Permanent removal from the community.

Responses should be proportional to the severity and frequency of the behavior.
